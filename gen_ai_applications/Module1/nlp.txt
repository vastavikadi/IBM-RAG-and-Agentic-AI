- Natural Language Processing (NLP) is a subfield of artificial intelligence that focuses on the interaction between computers and human language. It involves the development of algorithms and models that enable machines to understand, interpret, and generate human language in a way that is both meaningful and useful. NLP combines computational linguistics with machine learning, deep learning, and statistical modeling to process and analyze large amounts of natural language data.

- It consists of NLU and NLG. NLU (Natural Language Understanding) focuses on comprehending the meaning and context of human language, while NLG (Natural Language Generation) is concerned with producing human-like text based on given input. NLP applications include chatbots, sentiment analysis, language translation, and information retrieval, among others. By leveraging NLP techniques, businesses can enhance customer interactions, automate content creation, and gain insights from unstructured text data.

- First stage of NLP is tokenization, which involves breaking down text into smaller units called tokens. Tokens can be words, phrases, or even characters, depending on the level of granularity required for the analysis. Tokenization is a crucial step in NLP as it allows models to process and analyze text data more effectively. Once the text is tokenized, various NLP techniques can be applied to extract meaning, identify patterns, and generate responses.

- Second stage is stemming and lemmatization, which are techniques used to reduce words to their base or root forms. Stemming involves removing suffixes from words to obtain the stem, while lemmatization considers the context and converts words to their dictionary form (lemma). These processes help in normalizing text data, reducing redundancy, and improving the performance of NLP models by ensuring that different forms of a word are treated as a single entity. Examples of stemming and lemmatization include converting "running" to "run" or "better" to "good." By applying these techniques, NLP models can better understand the underlying meaning of text and provide more accurate analyses and responses. Example of Lemmatization: "better" -> "good", "running" -> "run", "children" -> "child". Example of Stemming: "running" -> "run", "happiness" -> "happi", "fishing" -> "fish". Stemming removes suffixes and may not produce actual words, while lemmatization produces valid words by considering the context and meaning of the word, searching their dictionary form.

- Next stage is part of speech tagging, which involves assigning grammatical categories (such as nouns, verbs, adjectives, etc.) to each token in a text. This process helps in understanding the syntactic structure of sentences and provides valuable information for further analysis. Part of speech tagging is essential for tasks like named entity recognition, sentiment analysis, and machine translation, as it allows models to identify relationships between words and their roles within a sentence.

- Next stage is Named Entity Recognition (NER), which is a subtask of NLP that focuses on identifying and classifying named entities in text into predefined categories such as person names, organizations, locations, dates, and more. NER is crucial for extracting structured information from unstructured text data, enabling applications like information retrieval, question answering, and knowledge graph construction. By recognizing named entities, NLP models can better understand the context and meaning of text, leading to more accurate analyses and responses.
